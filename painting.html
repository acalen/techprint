Painting

Spray painting is a technique where a device sprays a coating (paint, ink, varnich, etc.) throught the air onto a surface. Most common types emply compressed gas to atomize and direct the paint particles.

Two component paints offer optimal resistance to chemicals and abrasions, as found in various epoxies. Since they incorporate cross-linking chemical reactions between a base (part A) and a catalyst (part B), they must be mixed correctly to ensure high performance.

Powder coating is a type of coating that is applied as a free flowing, dry powder. The coating is typically applied electrostatically and is then cured under heat to allow it to flow and form a skin. Powder coating is mainly used for the coating of metals.

Key Benefits

  • Water based coatings will have fewer VOCs than solvent based coatings
  • Two component paints offer optimal resistance to chemicals and abrasions
  • Powder coating is one of the most economical, longest lasting, and color-durable quality finishes on any type of metal
