Hot Stamping

Hot stamping is a dry painting method where pre-dried ink or foils are transferred to the surface of a part at high temperatures. This method can be used to decorate a variety of products made from plastic, wood, leather, cardboard, and paper. A wide range of colors, including metallic and wood grains, are also available for this method.

During the process of hot stamping, a die is mounted and heated. The product to be decorated is placed beneath the die with a foil or mylar carrier containing the desired color between the two. The die presses down through the foil or mylar carrier and impresses the dry paint into the surface of the product.

Heat transfers are applied with a hot stamping press using a photo EXE as a register so the decoration is applied in the correct location. Heat transfers offer multi-color decoration in a single application, this eliminates multiple setups needed for other multi-color printing methods.

Shapes that lend themselves to the hot stamping and heat transfer methods include flats, slightly conical and simple compound curves, smooth surfaces, light textures, multilevel configurations, and raised graphics.

Key Benefits

  • Capable of printing on a large variety of materials and objects
  • Dry printing method eliminates the need for complicated ink mixtures
  • This process allows for branding as a failsafe should the ink ever fade
